## How to use this guide

Work top to bottom, one era at a time, and tick things off as you go. The boxes remember your progress, so you can disappear for a month and pick up where you left off.

{{% callout tone="tip" title="The only rule" %}}
No skipping to the hits. The album tracks are where the real ones live.
{{% /callout %}}

## Era one: the duo years

Start where the mixtapes started. Two albums, an unreasonable number of singles, and the b-sides that started this whole site.

{{% checklist key="lg-duo" %}}
Listen to the debut album front to back, no shuffle.
Listen to Affirmation front to back, ideally on a road trip.
Find your favourite non-single album track and defend it to a friend.
Watch one live performance from the era and note the wardrobe.
{{% /checklist %}}

## Era two: going solo

The reinvention records. Braver, stranger, and the reason the word "underrated" appears forty-one times on this blog.

{{% checklist key="lg-solo" %}}
Listen to the first solo record in one sitting.
Listen to the electronic one with headphones, in the dark, as intended.
Pick a b-side from this era for your own mixtape.
Read one contemporary review and get retroactively annoyed at it.
{{% /checklist %}}

## Era three: the return

The later records, made on nobody's schedule but his own. Start here if you want to hear an artist entirely at ease.

{{% checklist key="lg-return" %}}
Listen to the comeback record twice, it opens up on the second pass.
Rank your top five tracks across all eras and email them to me.
Attend a show, or watch a full concert film, and report back.
{{% /checklist %}}

{{% callout tone="info" title="Done with all three?" %}}
Congratulations, you are now legally obliged to have opinions about track sequencing. The [news feed](/blog/) will keep you supplied with fresh arguments.
{{% /callout %}}
