## 1 · Book a venue

Lock the room before anything else, the date depends on it. See the handbook's [venue criteria](/handbook/) for what makes a good space.

{{% checklist key="rb-venue" %}}
Shortlist 2–3 possible venues from our host contacts.
Confirm capacity (20–40), step-free access and an accessible washroom.
Confirm the equipment and setup your format needs.
Agree the date, start/end time and who holds the key.
Visit or verify the room in person.
Get the host's day-of phone number and building access details.
Send the host a written confirmation with date, time and headcount.
{{% /checklist %}}

## 2 · Line up a session host

A confirmed venue makes it easy to invite a host. Remember: first-timers welcome, offer to help them prepare.

{{% checklist key="rb-host" %}}
Invite a session host (or two), check our list of members with topics they love.
Confirm the session title and format (talk / hands-on / show & tell).
Collect a 2–3 sentence intro and a photo for promotion.
Share the run of show and expected attendance.
For hands-on nights: confirm any supplies or prep to tell attendees in advance.
Offer a rehearsal or co-hosting to first-timers.
Send a calendar invite with venue address and arrival time.
{{% /checklist %}}

{{% callout tone="info" title="No host this month?" %}}
Run a show & tell or a social. A dependable rhythm matters more than a packed agenda.
{{% /callout %}}

## 3 · Set up the event page

Your RSVP platform is the single source of truth. Create the event once all the details are firm.

{{% checklist key="rb-page" %}}
Create the event with title, date and time.
Write a short, warm description: what, who it's for, and that everyone is welcome.
Add the venue address and accessibility notes.
Set a sensible capacity matching the room.
Add the host's name, session title and intro.
Upload the event image.
Turn on a waitlist and a reminder email.
{{% /checklist %}}

## 4 · Promote the event

Post early, remind often. Use the [communications guidance](/handbook/) for channels and timing.

{{% checklist key="rb-promote" %}}
Announce ~2 weeks out across your channels.
Make the poster with your template.
Post a reminder ~3 days before with the RSVP link.
Post a "tonight!" nudge on the day of the event.
Thank confirmed sponsors/hosts in the announcements.
Ask the session host to share the event with their network.
{{% /checklist %}}

## 5 · Day-of run of show

Arrive early, set a warm tone, and keep to time so everyone catches transit home.

{{% checklist key="rb-dayof" %}}
Arrive 45–60 minutes early; set up the room and test any equipment.
Set up the welcome table and put up signage from the entrance.
Lay out snacks and drinks.
Greet every arrival; give newcomers a warm, personal hello.
Open with a brief Code of Conduct reminder and housekeeping.
Introduce the session host; keep things to time.
Take a few photos: always ask consent first.
Thank everyone, announce next month's date, and close up safely.
{{% /checklist %}}

{{% callout tone="warn" title="Safety first, always" %}}
You have the authority to pause an activity or ask someone to leave if behaviour makes others unsafe. See the [Code of Conduct](/code-of-conduct/).
{{% /callout %}}

## 6 · Post-event recap

A tidy wrap-up keeps hosts happy and the community in the loop.

{{% checklist key="rb-recap" %}}
Leave the venue cleaner than you found it; thank the host in person.
Send thank-you notes to hosts and sponsors within 48 hours.
Pick a few consented photos for the recap.
Write and publish a short recap post with shout-outs.
Log what worked and what didn't in the organizer notes.
Confirm next month's date and venue.
{{% /checklist %}}

{{% callout tone="tip" title="Better late than never" %}}
If the recap slips, publish it anyway, our community would rather read a late one than none at all.
{{% /callout %}}
