Popular is here: a warm, community-first theme for meetups, clubs and fan communities, shipping for **Hugo** and **Astro** on day one, in parity, from the same design system.

## What's in the box

- **Events, done right**: upcoming and past split automatically, with speaker profile cards, venue pages that carry arrival notes (buzz codes and all), check-in instructions, RSVP buttons and a "venue wanted" state
- **An organizer docs area**: handbook and runbooks with a scroll-tracking table of contents and checklists that remember your progress in the browser
- **A multi-author blog**: tag filtering, pagination, author profiles, inline guest writers
- **A composable home page**: hero, stats, features, member testimonials and a closing call to action, all from config
- **Batteries included**: one command imports your Sessionize schedule or your spreadsheet plan, cross-referenced and dependency-free
- **Agent & human friendly**: `AGENTS.md` is included, so AI coding agents follow the same rules as people
- **One-block re-theming**: change `brand.primary` and the whole site follows, derived tints included; accessibility is enforced in CI, starting with required alt text

## Four demos, zero tricks

Popular ships with four complete fictional example sites: **Rocky Cove Aquarium Club** (teal), **Lucky Town Foodie Club** (copper), **KDrama Fan Club** (indigo), and **Truly Madly Riley** (gold, a personal site for one devoted superfan). Diff any two configs and you'll find the entire visual difference: a `brand` block, nav labels and copy. Zero template changes, zero CSS.

Everything in them is fictional on purpose, every link points to `example.com`, so you can safely copy a demo, search-and-replace, and publish. [Browse the demos](/docs/demos/) and pick the closest vibe.

## Communities first, but it flexes

The theme is built for communities, and two other shapes fall out for free. This very website is Popular running as a **product site** (docs, blog, no events). Truly Madly Riley shows it as a **personal site** (the blog as a news feed, events as an influencer's appearances, no organizers page). Sections you don't create content for simply never render.

## Get started

The [quick start](/docs/quick-start/) takes about five minutes for either framework. If Popular makes your community site easier, star the repo and tell another organizer about it.
